## Amazon.es FBA Fee Structure (2026)

Amazon.es uses the same EU FBA fee schedule. Fees below reflect 2025–2026 structure (updated March 2024, stable into 2026).

### Fee Reference: Key Definitions
- **Referral fee**: Category-based percentage of sale price (incl. VAT charged to buyer, but Amazon calculates referral on price ex-VAT in Spain — however listings show VAT-inclusive prices; referral is on total sale price paid by customer)
- **FBA fulfillment fee**: Based on product size tier and weight
- **Storage fee**: Per cubic foot per month (Amazon uses cubic feet internally; billed per unit in practice)
- **Inbound shipping**: Your cost to ship goods from supplier to Amazon FBA warehouse (Amazon Logistics / freight forwarder)

### Size Tier Assumptions for a Typical Patent-Mining Product
A garden tool or kitchen gadget (e.g. 200g, 20×10×3 cm):
- **Size tier**: Small Standard (fits within 30×20×5 cm, ≤1 kg)
- **FBA fulfillment fee (small standard, ≤500g)**: **€3.22** per unit (2025–2026 EU rate)

## First Inventory Order Budget

### Scenario Analysis (Validated Winning Product, €1.60 COGS at 500 units)

**Assumptions**:
- Sale price: €14.99
- Net margin per unit (before PPC): €7.34
- PPC cost per unit sold: €1.50
- True net per unit: €5.84
- Break-even = total upfront COGS + shipping ÷ net margin per unit

| Scenario | Units | Unit cost (€) | Sea freight China→Spain | Total COGS (units + shipping) | Net margin/unit (after FBA, before PPC) | Break-even units (excl. PPC) | Break-even units (incl. PPC) | Break-even revenue |
|----------|-------|---------------|------------------------|------------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------------|-------------------|
| Conservative | 300 | €1.80 | €200 | €740 | €7.19 | 103 | 127 | **€1,904** |
| Recommended | 500 | €1.60 | €280 | €1,080 | €7.34 | 147 | 185 | **€2,774** |
| Aggressive | 1,000 | €1.30 | €400 | €1,700 | €7.64 | 222 | 278 | **€4,167** |

**Calculation notes**:
- Conservative: 300 × €1.80 = €540 + €200 shipping = **€740**
- Recommended: 500 × €1.60 = €800 + €280 shipping = **€1,080**
- Aggressive: 1,000 × €1.30 = €1,300 + €400 shipping = **€1,700**
- Sea freight China→Spain: typically €0.50–0.70/kg for LCL (Less than Container Load). A 500-unit order of small goods (~150 kg gross) costs €250–350 door-to-door including customs clearance and Amazon FBA prep. €280 is realistic mid-case.
- Break-even (excl. PPC) = Total COGS ÷ gross margin per unit
- Break-even (incl. PPC) = Total COGS ÷ true net per unit (after PPC)

> **CFO recommendation**: Start with the **Recommended (500 units)** scenario. The conservative 300-unit order has higher per-unit cost and lower supplier leverage. The aggressive 1,000-unit order ties up too much capital before demand is validated. At 185 units to break-even (37% of inventory), break-even is achievable in Month 2–3.

## CFO Recommendation

### Starting Capital Summary
- **Minimum viable starting budget: €1,800** (conservative 300-unit order, 2 months PPC, no buffer)
- **Recommended starting budget: €2,711** (500-unit order, 3-month PPC, 15% buffer)
- **If capital is constrained**: Start with Phase 1 only (€450), validate one product via FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant) before committing to FBA inventory. This extends timeline by 4–6 weeks but reduces capital at risk to near zero until demand is proven.

### One-Way Doors Requiring CEO Approval Before Spending

The following are **irreversible or high-commitment** financial decisions that must not be executed autonomously:

1. **Placing the first bulk inventory order (>€500)** — once goods are manufactured and shipped, funds are committed. CEO approves after sample validation confirms demand signal.
2. **Scaling PPC above €15/day (€450/month)** — budget threshold crossed; requires CEO sign-off on ACoS performance data.
3. **Reorder of >1,000 units or second SKU launch** — major capital commitment; CEO reviews Month 4+ P&L before approving.
4. **Autónomo / SL company registration in Spain** — legal commitment with recurring social security costs (~€230–290/month); only justified once revenue exceeds €1,500/month.
5. **Trademark / Brand Registry application** — ~€850 EU trademark fee; one-way spend; CEO approves based on sales velocity.
6. **Third-party logistics (3PL) contract for FBA prep** — ongoing service commitment; review at 500+ units/month.
7. **Any single supplier payment exceeding €2,000** — wire transfer risk; requires dual review and Trade Assurance verification.
